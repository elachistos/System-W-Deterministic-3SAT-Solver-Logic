The procedure can be integrated with CDCL-style solvers by treating System W saturation as a theory oracle. Rather than encoding the entire formula at once, the solver samples a subset of the initial assumptions, runs saturation over that subset, and checks for ⊥. If no contradiction is found, additional assumptions are added and saturation continues from the current state, inheriting all previously derived sentences. This incremental approach mirrors CDCL's conflict-driven learning — each saturation step accumulates consequences that persist and constrain all future steps, allowing the procedure to generalize efficiently across large formula instances.
